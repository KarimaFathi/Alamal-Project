Our Mission

We are providing a safe life for disabled children in a way that grants them the chance to develop their skills and abilities, and rely on themselves in the daily life.

About

Dura Al Amal Charitable Association

Our local charitable humanitarian non-profit association, established in 2018 and registered under number DR-592-M, follows the Ministry of Social Development's law on charitable organizations. We established the Dura Al-Amal Center to provide habilitation, learning services, and behavior modification for disabled children, focusing on their psychological and social inclusion in schools and society.

Dr. Mohammad T. Alsayyed Ahmad

Four years ago, the Dura Al-Amal Charitable Association (DACA) was established to provide various health services. They started by creating the Dura Al-Amal Center for Rehabilitation (DACR) for children with special needs. With support from local and international donors, DACR has improved its services significantly. While they have met interim goals, they aim to establish an AUTISM Center and a Rehabilitation Hospital. Thanks to all who contributed and donated. God bless you all.

Acitivities

The Educational Program

The center provides educational services within remedial classes, adopting the kindergarten curriculum, special education, and learning difficulties through special education teachers.
The association develops an appropriate educational curriculum for these children to focus on basic information and life skills.
The center sets rehabilitation plans for children and develops evaluation protocols for them in the center.

The Professional Program

The association provides the necessary services for the vocational preparatory class, from the age of 14 years and over, in the field of making candles, gypsum molds, flower nesting, soap making, drawing, and wood carving.
The association is currently seeking to develop students' skills according to their capabilities and abilities to use the available CNC machine.

The Economic Program

The association secures and provides resources through:
- Nominal student tuition fees.
- Fees for rehabilitative treatment sessions.
- Sales of vocational preparatory class products (soap, accessories, wax, CNC-machined wooden items) at exhibitions and bazaars.
- Donations and zakat from the local community.
- A Sponsorship or Kafalat project for student premiums and treatment, starting from 100 shekels (about $30) or more.
The association aims to expand its services to support the Center and its mission.
